Digital Tides

3 Strategic Moves Remaking Regional Connectivity

Recent subsea cable projects highlight a new era of digital sovereignty, resilience, and massive capacity growth in the Middle East, all happening within a few weeks.

The Three Key Moves

SEA-ME-WE 6: Dual Landing in Egypt

July 3, 2025

What: Telecom Egypt & SubCom completed dual landings at Port Said and Ras Ghareb, creating two terrestrial routes.

Impact: Dramatically boosts resiliency for Europe-Middle East traffic and reduces Suez chokepoints.

2AFRICA: Bahrain's Capacity Boost

June 4, 2025

What: A $205M investment connecting Bahrain to the world's longest cable (45,000 km at 180 Tbps).

Impact: Quintuples (5x) Bahrain's international capacity, powering its fintech and cloud ambitions.

KHALEEJ NORTH: New Regional Link

July 16, 2025

What: Beyon Group & SubCom to build an 800 km cable connecting Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, and Iraq.

Impact: Positions Bahrain as a nexus for data sovereignty, low-latency backhaul, and disaster-proof routing.

What This Really Means: The Big Picture

Redundancy is King

Even established corridors are doubling up on landings and crossings to eliminate single points of failure and guarantee uptime.

Capacity Wars Are Heating Up

Terabit-scale throughput is now the minimum to support 5G, AI workloads, and hyperscaler cloud services.

The Regional Hub Play

Nations like Bahrain and Egypt are strategically moving from simple transit points to indispensable digital crossroads.

The Bottom Line

These aren't incremental upgrades—they are strategic bets on digital sovereignty, resilience, and scale.
